⚠ Flex Duct Critical Notes
  • Flex duct loses 15–35% efficiency when kinked or compressed — always fully extend.
  • Max recommended flex run: 6 ft for branch takeoffs; avoid long trunk flex runs.
  • Insulated flex R-6 is the residential standard; R-8 required in attics per IECC 2021.
  • Never use flex as a main trunk — use rigid sheet metal for all trunk lines.

⚡ Pro Tips — Duct Sizing
  • Equal Friction Method: Design at 0.08 in. w.g./100 ft for most residential systems.
  • Target velocities: Supply mains 700–900 FPM · Branch ducts 500–700 FPM · Returns 400–600 FPM.
  • Oversizing is a trap: Low velocity = poor throw, condensation risk, comfort complaints.
  • Aspect ratio: Keep rectangular ducts at 3:1 or less; 4:1 maximum per ACCA Manual D.

🏆 Residential Target Most residential systems are rated for 0.50 in. w.g. TESP. Design your duct system to use no more than 80% of this budget.
🔧 High Static Symptoms High TESP causes reduced airflow, poor comfort, coil freeze-up, compressor strain, and noise. Measure and solve — don't just set a higher fan speed.
