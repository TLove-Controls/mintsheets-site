
Section 1 — When an HVAC Permit Is Required
Three tiered checkbox grids covering: Always Required (12 work types), May Be Required / Verify with AHJ (10 types), and Typically NOT Required (10 types). Plus a separate EPA 608 notice box clarifying that refrigerant handling is federally regulated independent of local permits.
Section 2 — Contractor Information (Pre-Filled)
16-row info table with all contractor credentials in one place: company name, DBA, owner, addresses, phone/email, AR contractor license # and expiration, EPA 608 certification number, GL insurance policy/expiration, Workers' Comp policy/expiration, bond information, and Federal EIN.
Section 3 — Residential HVAC Permit Application
Full 8-part residential permit form:

Part A — Project/property info (address, parcel, property type checkboxes, year built, square footage)
Part B — Property owner info
Part C — Scope of work checkboxes (new install, replacement, duct only, gas, electrical) + description text area
Part D — Equipment specifications (16 fields: existing equipment, new manufacturer/model/serial, system type, fuel source, heating/cooling capacity, SEER2/HSPF2, refrigerant type and charge, electrical supply, gas line, duct type, blower type)
Part E — Load calculation attachment status + software/version fields
Part F — Installation location checkboxes (outdoor unit: ground/rooftop/bracket; indoor: attic/crawl/basement/closet/garage/utility)
Part G — Estimated valuation, permit fee, start/completion dates
Part H — Contractor certification statement and signature

Section 4 — Commercial HVAC Permit Application
5-part commercial form with occupancy classification checkboxes (B/M/A/S/I/F/E/R-2), system type checkboxes (VAV/VRF/RTU/chilled water/boiler/DOAS/commercial refrigeration), 6-row equipment schedule table (Tag #, Equipment Type, Manufacturer/Model, Capacity, Voltage, Location), and full code compliance matrix (building code, mechanical code, energy code, ventilation standard, design conditions, compliance method, PE stamp requirement).
Section 5 — Pre-Submittal Checklist
Three tiers: General (13 items, Yes/N/A columns), Residential Additional (6 items), Commercial Additional (8 items) — all with checkbox columns.
Section 6 — Inspection Readiness Checklist
Two tiers: Rough-In (17 items: permit posted, drawings on-site, refrigerant line test, condensate, electrical disconnect, wire gauge, duct installation, combustion air, flue/venting, gas pressure test) and Final (15 items: system operational, refrigerant charge log, thermostat modes, access panels, condensate test, equipment clearances, duct sealing, CO detector, customer walkthrough). Full Pass/Fail/N/A columns. Technician sign-off line.
Section 7 — Northwest Arkansas AHJ Quick Reference
13-row jurisdiction table: Rogers, Bentonville, Fayetteville, Springdale, Lowell, Centerton, Bella Vista, Pea Ridge, Cave Springs, Gravette, Siloam Springs, Benton County unincorporated, Washington County unincorporated — with phone numbers, online portal status, permit requirement status (Required/Verify), and department notes. Green/amber color coding for status.
Section 8 — Permit Log & Tracking
12-row active permit tracking table (Permit #, Project Address, Customer Name, Type, Submitted, Approved, Rough-In, Final, Closed) with log maintainer and review date sign-off line.
Acknowledgment — Field Supervisor/PM signature confirming permit responsibility.
