What This Calculator Does

The Service Price Calculator allows you to build a complete diagnostic and repair quote. It factors in travel fees, technician hourly rates, and multi-line parts lists. Streamlining HVAC Service Pricing

The HVAC Service Price Calculator is engineered to convert raw field data—labor hours, part costs, and travel mileage—into a polished, customer-facing flat-rate price. Standardizing your service pricing is critical for scaling a mechanical contracting business, as it removes the guesswork for technicians and ensures every truck rolls with the same profitability targets.

Utilizing flat-rate pricing via Section 5 over traditional Time and Materials (T&M) protects your bottom line. When technicians work quickly due to their high skill level, T&M billing penalizes them by charging the customer for fewer hours. Flat-rate pricing rewards efficiency, allowing your best techs to complete more calls per day while maintaining a high billing rate. It also provides the customer with upfront pricing certainty, drastically reducing invoice disputes.

Protecting Profit on Every Repair

When calculating the final ticket, variables like After-Hours Surcharges, Membership Discounts, and true equipment costs must be factored flawlessly. Section 3 of this tool ensures that an emergency midnight call calculates the multiplier on labor properly, while Section 6 gives the service manager a transparent view of the final breakdown (Labor vs. Parts vs. Overhead vs. Net Profit).

For larger jobs that drift beyond basic service into full equipment replacement, transition your sales process over to the HVAC bid calculator to handle the heavy equipment costs. If you are struggling to lock in the correct multipliers to use in this tool, refer back to the profit margin calculator to double-check that your overhead percentage is properly calibrated.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I use flat rate or time and materials?

Flat rate pricing is generally superior for HVAC service. It provides the customer with upfront certainty and rewards your technicians for their efficiency and expertise. T&M often leads to "clock-watching" and billing disputes.

How do I calculate my hourly break-even rate?

Your break-even rate is the total of all non-billable overhead expenses divided by your billable hours. You must then add your desired profit margin on top of this rate to settle on your final billable hourly rate in the calculator.
