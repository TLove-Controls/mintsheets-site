HVAC Sizing Tools: Get the Right System Every Time

Correct system sizing is the most important technical decision in any HVAC project. An undersized system runs continuously without achieving setpoint during peak demand. An oversized system short-cycles, fails to dehumidify, and experiences premature mechanical failure. Both scenarios result in customer callbacks, poor reviews, and potential warranty disputes.

Our HVAC sizing tools guide you through the correct four-step sizing workflow: (1) calculate the building's heating and cooling load using the HVAC load calculator; (2) determine total system airflow and room-level CFM requirements using the CFM calculator and airflow per room tool; (3) design the duct system using the duct sizing calculator; and (4) verify the total external static pressure budget with the static pressure calculator.

For ductless mini split applications, the mini split sizing calculator handles multi-zone BTU capacity planning with climate zone adjustments. Our blog guide How to Size an HVAC System Properly explains the complete process in depth.
