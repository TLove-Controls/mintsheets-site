Product FAQ

What is a sliding scale markup?
A sliding scale uses higher multipliers for cheaper parts. For example, a $2 capacitor might need a 6x multiplier (selling for $12), while a $3,000 furnace might only use a 1.5x multiplier. This spreadsheet organizes that math for you.
Can I edit the price brackets?
Yes. The spreadsheet logic is unprotected, allowing you to tweak the brackets ($0-$50, $51-$100, etc.) and multipliers to perfectly fit your local market conditions.
